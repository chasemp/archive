devops type thoughts

Random...

First step of any project: cleary define your deliverables. Final step: deliver them. Modular thought is reusable thought; modular systems are reusable systems. Temporary solutions do not exist; if it is a solution, design it to be (at least semi-) permanent. Security heartaches now, or later, but sooner than later is good. Security is not a noun. Self contained systems are more manageable. The more a device claims to do, the fewer things it will do really well. 'On the roadmap' or 'in the next release' mean less than nothing unless put in writing. A duck takes off from a lake: a sportsman shoots his trophie. Understand change impact. Without a baseline troubleshooting is nearly impossible. Documentation is your baseline. Two things that should be automated: logging of changes, and backups. Audit access to both. Knowing you don't know is better than thinking you know; and not knowing. The bold print giveth and the fine print taketh away. Read it again. Weak minds want to be surrounded by weaker minds. Strong minds the opposite. Cheap, Fast, Right. Cheap and Fast will rarely be Right. draft...
